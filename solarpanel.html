Solar Panels

Solar panels are devices that convert sunlight into electricity. They provide clean, renewable energy, reduce reliance on fossil fuels, and help combat climate change. With minimal maintenance, solar panels can produce electricity for over 25 years, offering long-term cost savings. They can be installed on rooftops or in large-scale solar farms, providing a decentralized energy solution. Solar panels are a promising pathway to a sustainable future.

  1. The energy production of solar panels depends on factors such as sunlight intensity, panel efficiency, and installation conditions.
  2. On average, a typical solar panel with an area of about 1 square meter can produce between 150 to 300 watts of power under direct sunlight.
  3. Therefore, solar panels can produce approximately 1.5 to 3 kilowatt-hours (kWh) of electricity per day under optimal conditions.
  4. It's important to note that energy production varies throughout the day and is lower during cloudy or shaded periods.

Cost:

  1. The cost of solar panels has decreased over the years. On average, the installation cost for residential solar panels can range from $2,500 to $8,000 per kilowatt (kW) of capacity. Therefore, a typical 5 kW residential solar system may cost between $12,500 to $40,000.
  2. Utility-scale solar projects can have lower costs due to economies of scale. The cost per installed kilowatt for large-scale solar installations can range from $1,000 to $3,000, depending on the project size and location.
