Windmills

Windmills, or wind turbines, generate clean electricity by harnessing the power of wind. They are eco-friendly, scalable, and provide long-term cost benefits. With no fuel costs and low operational expenses, windmills offer a sustainable energy solution that reduces reliance on fossil fuels. They contribute to a greener future and bring economic opportunities to local communities. Wind energy is a viable and renewable option for meeting our growing electricity needs..

  1. The energy production of wind turbines depends on the wind speed and the turbine's size and efficiency.
  2. On average, a modern utility-scale wind turbine with a capacity of 2 to 3 megawatts (MW) can produce around 5 to 8 million kWh of electricity annually.
  3. This translates to an average energy production of approximately 570 to 950 kWh per hour, assuming a constant wind speed.
  4. However, wind turbine energy production is not constant and depends on wind availability and speed variations.

    5. Cost:

    1. The cost of wind turbines varies depending on their size, capacity, and location. Onshore wind turbines typically cost around $1,000 to $2,500 per kilowatt (kW) of capacity. Offshore wind farms tend to be more expensive, with costs ranging from $3,000 to $6,000 per kilowatt (kW).
    2. The total project costs also include expenses for land acquisition, infrastructure, transmission lines, and installation, which can significantly impact the overall cost.
